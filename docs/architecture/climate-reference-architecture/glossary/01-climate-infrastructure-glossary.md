## 1. The Canonical Language

The CIOS operates across multiple domains (software, finance, science, government). To prevent architectural drift, the following definitions are canonical and must be used consistently across all code, databases, APIs, and UIs within VeriField Nexus.

## 2. Core Entities

### Activity
A discrete human or physical event that occurs to maintain or operate an Asset or Project (e.g., a maintenance visit, a community consultation). Activities generate Evidence.

### Asset
A tangible physical unit deployed within a Project. (e.g., Cookstove SN: 10492, Solar Panel SN: 9948, Hectare of Forest). Generates Telemetry.

### Evidence
The raw, atomic proof points generated by Assets or Activities (e.g., a geo-tagged photo, an MQTT telemetry payload). Must be cryptographically hashed upon ingestion.

### Jurisdiction
A bounded spatial polygon (often aligning with political boundaries like a Country or State) where specific National or Regulated rules apply. Projects inherit rules from the Jurisdiction they intersect with.

### Programme (PoA - Programme of Activities)
A methodological and financial umbrella framework spanning a large geography, authorizing multiple specific Projects (CPAs) to be executed under a unified rule set over time.

### Project (CPA - Component Project Activity)
A physically and temporally bounded execution of climate action. The primary entity that undergoes Registration, Auditing, and eventual Issuance.

### Portfolio
A logical grouping of Programmes and standalone Projects owned by an Investor or Developer for reporting and financial aggregation purposes. Generates no credits itself.

## 3. Climate Market Terminology

### Additionality
The foundational concept of carbon markets. A project is "additional" if the greenhouse gas emission reductions would *not* have occurred in the absence of the carbon finance revenue. If the project was financially viable without carbon credits, it is not additional.

### Baseline
The hypothetical scenario representing the greenhouse gas emissions that would have occurred in the absence of the proposed project activity. Carbon credits are calculated as: `Baseline Emissions - Project Emissions - Leakage`.

### Buffer Pool
A non-tradable reserve of Carbon Credits held back by a Registry (typically 10-20% of total issuance). Used as an insurance mechanism. If a project suffers an unintentional reversal (e.g., a forest burns down), credits are canceled from the buffer pool to maintain environmental integrity.

### Carbon Credit / Verified Emission Reduction (VER)
A tradable certificate representing the prevention, reduction, or removal of one metric tonne of carbon dioxide equivalent (1 tCO2e) from the atmosphere.

### Corresponding Adjustment (CA)
A mechanism under Article 6 of the Paris Agreement. If Country A sells a carbon credit to Country B (an ITMO), Country A must add 1 tCO2e back to its national ledger (an upward adjustment), and Country B subtracts 1 tCO2e (a downward adjustment) to prevent double-counting.

### Issuance
The act of minting a verified Carbon Credit on the ultimate ledger of record.

### Leakage
Unintended increases in greenhouse gas emissions outside a project's boundary that occur as a direct result of the project activities. (e.g., Protecting Forest A causes illegal loggers to move to Forest B). Leakage must be deducted from the total credits issued.

### Methodology
The approved scientific formula, parameter list, and monitoring requirements used to calculate the baseline and project emissions. 

### Monitoring Period (Vintage)
The specific temporal window (e.g., Jan 1 2024 to Dec 31 2024) during which evidence was collected and emission reductions occurred. The resulting credits bear this "Vintage".

### Permanence
The guarantee that the removed carbon will remain out of the atmosphere for a specified period (often 100 years). Reversals of permanence require buffer pool cancellations.

### Retirement
The final state of a Carbon Credit. When a corporate buyer claims the credit against their own emissions footprint, the credit is permanently burned and can never be traded again.

## 4. Verification Terminology

### Validation
The *ex-ante* (forward-looking) assessment of a Project Design Document (PDD) by an independent auditor to ensure it meets methodology rules before the project starts.

### Verification
The *ex-post* (backward-looking) periodic assessment of collected Evidence by an independent auditor to confirm that the claimed emission reductions actually occurred.

### Validation and Verification Body (VVB)
An independent, third-party auditing organization accredited by a standard body (e.g., UNFCCC, Verra) to perform validation and verification services.
